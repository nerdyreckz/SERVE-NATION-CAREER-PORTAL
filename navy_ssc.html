Short Service Commission Officer Entry
What is Navy SSC?

Short Service Commission (SSC) is a direct officer entry program for college graduates and postgraduates who want to serve in the Indian Navy. It's designed for candidates who are ready for leadership roles but want a defined service period.

Most SSC entries don't have a written exam. Candidates are shortlisted based on their academic marks, and the selection happens through an SSB interview and medical tests.

Major branches under Navy SSC:
• IT (Information Technology)
• Observer (Air Operations)
• Logistics
• General Service (GS/X)
• Pilot (Aviation)
• Air Traffic Controller
• Education
• Law & Other Specialties

Who Conducts: Indian Navy (Ministry of Defence)

When: Usually twice a year (notifications released on official website)

Mode of Selection: Shortlisting → SSB Interview → Medical Exam → Final Merit

Training Academy: Indian Naval Academy (INA), Ezhimala, Kerala

Commission Type: Short Service Commission (typically 10–14 years of service)
Eligibility Criteria

General Requirements:
  • Indian citizen (male and female eligible for most branches)
  • Medically fit as per Navy standards

IT Branch: B.Tech/B.E. (Computer Science, IT, Electronics) | Age: 20–28 years | Both genders eligible

Pilot & Observer: B.Sc (PCM) or B.Tech | Age: 19–24 years | Must meet aviation medical standards | Nationals only (generally)

Air Traffic Controller: B.Tech (Electronics/Electrical) or B.Sc | Age: 20–25 years

Logistics & Education: Any graduate stream | Age: 20–27 years | Both genders eligible

General Service (GS/X): Any undergraduate degree | Age: 21–27 years | Male candidates only
Selection Process
Step 1: Shortlisting
Candidates are shortlisted based on academic marks, degree, and branch requirements.
Step 2: SSB Interview
5-day assessment at any SSB center covering group exercises, personal interviews, psychological tests, and outdoor tasks.
Step 3: Medical Examination
Comprehensive medical tests at authorized Navy hospitals to check fitness.
Step 4: Final Merit List
Final selection based on SSB recommendation, academic performance, and medical clearance.
Training

Training Academy: Indian Naval Academy (INA), Ezhimala, Kerala

Duration: Approximately 22 weeks (standard) or as per branch requirements

Training Modules Include:
  • Seamanship and naval operations
  • Physical training and combat fitness
  • Swimming and water survival
  • Navigation and maritime basics
  • Discipline, leadership, and officer etiquette
  • Branch-specific technical training
Career Path & Promotions

Starting Rank: Sub-Lieutenant (junior officer commission)

Promotion Ladder: Sub-Lieutenant → Lieutenant → Lieutenant Commander → Commander → Captain → Rear Admiral (and beyond)

Nature of Work: Officers manage operations, lead teams, handle technical systems, and contribute to national defense based on their branch.

Additional Benefits: Sea allowances, flying allowances (for pilots), pension, medical coverage, and career growth opportunities.
