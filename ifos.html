Indian Forest Service (IFoS)
Guardians of India’s Forests, Wildlife & Natural Heritage

"The Earth does not belong to us; we belong to the Earth."

Basic Information About the Exam

  • Conducted by: UPSC
  • All India Service (one of the 3 AIS)
  • Works with State Forest Departments & MoEFCC
  • Exam frequency: Annual
  • Mode: Offline
  • Age limits: 21–32 (General), 35 (OBC), 37 (SC/ST)
  • Science background required
  • Attempts: 6 (General), 9 (OBC), unlimited (SC/ST)
  • Notification: February
  • Prelims shared with CSE
  • Mains & Interview exclusive to IFoS
  • Exam centres: 70+

Result Criteria

Prelims: qualifying only

Mains: 6 descriptive papers, counted for merit

Interview: marks added to Mains

Final merit = Mains + Interview

Scope (Posts, Promotions, Salary, Work)

  • Initial Post: ACF / DCF
  • Cadre allocation: State-based
  • Promotion Ladder: DCF → CF → CCF → PCCF → DG Forests
  • Salary: Pay Level 10 onwards
  • Perks: residence, vehicle, staff, field allowances, forest rations
  • Nature of duties:
    • Wildlife protection
    • Forest conservation
    • Anti-poaching operations
    • Environment policy work
    • Field inspections & surveys
    • Community & tribal welfare

Syllabus

Prelims (Common with CSE):

  • GS Paper 1
  • CSAT

IFoS Mains:

  • General English
  • General Knowledge
  • Optional Subject Paper 1
  • Optional Subject Paper 2
  • Optional subjects: Agriculture, Forestry, Botany, Zoology, Geology, Maths, Physics, Chemistry, etc.

Exam Pattern / Selection Process

  1. Stage 1: Prelims
  2. Stage 2: IFoS Mains (6 descriptive papers)
  3. Stage 3: Interview
Paper Marks
Prelims200
Mains1200
Interview300

Training Period

  • Academy: IGNFA Dehradun
  • Duration: 1 year
  • Modules:
    • Forestry, wildlife, ecology
    • GIS & remote sensing
    • Environment law
    • Field training
    • Visits to national parks & tiger reserves
    • All-India study tours (incl. Winter Tour)
