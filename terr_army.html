Territorial Army
Officer Entry (Civilian Category)
What is the Territorial Army?

The Territorial Army is India's part-time military force where civilians serve as commissioned officers while maintaining their regular jobs. It's a unique opportunity to contribute to national security without leaving your career.

TA officers support the regular Army during national emergencies, internal security operations, and disaster relief efforts. Your role is both meaningful and flexible.
Basic Information You Need to Know

Conducting Authority: Ministry of Defence (Army)

Entry Type: For employed and self-employed civilians

Nature of Service: Voluntary, part-time commissioned officer role

Notification: Announced periodically throughout the year

Application: www.indianarmy.nic.in
Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: 18 to 42 years
  • Education: Bachelor's degree from a recognized university
  • Employment: Must be employed or self-employed
  • Physical Fitness: Medically and physically fit as per Army standards
  • Nationality: Indian citizen
Selection Process

  • Written Examination (for civilian candidates)
  • PIB Interview (Preliminary Interview Board)
  • SSB Interview (Service Selection Board)
  • Medical Examination
  • Final Merit List and Commissioning
Written Exam Pattern & Syllabus

Subjects Covered:

  • English
  • General Knowledge
  • Reasoning & Logical Ability
  • Elementary Mathematics

Difficulty Level: Moderate, designed for graduates with general awareness.

Note: Ex-servicemen candidates are generally exempt from the written exam.
Training

Selected officers undergo initial officer training at Army training centres across India. Post-commissioning, you attend an Annual Training Camp (2 weeks) and mandatory 2-month training every alternate year.

Training covers military drill, weapons handling, tactics, leadership development, and discipline—equipping you to lead effectively in the field.
Career Path, Ranks & Salary

Starting Rank: Lieutenant

Promotion Ladder: Lieutenant → Captain → Major → Lieutenant Colonel → Colonel (based on merit and service)

Salary: During embodied service, you earn the same as regular Army officers. Pay includes basic salary, allowances, and benefits.

You may be deployed during national emergencies and internal security operations, serving wherever needed.
