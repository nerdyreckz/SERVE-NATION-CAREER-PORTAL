TGC — Technical Graduate Course
Indian Army
"Engineering builds structures, discipline builds character, and leadership builds nations. The TGC is where all three meet."
1. Basic Information

The Technical Graduate Course (TGC) is a direct SSB entry opportunity designed specifically for engineering graduates who want to commission into the Indian Army. It's a straight path from your degree to becoming an officer.
  • Who can apply: Male B.Tech graduates from approved engineering branches
  • How often: Conducted twice a year by the Indian Army
  • Selection criteria: Based on your B.Tech marks (no written exam required)
  • Age limit: Between 20–27 years old
  • Eligibility: B.Tech in Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, Electronics, Signals, or similar approved branches
  • Where to apply: joinindianarmy.nic.in (official portal)
  • Outcome: Selected candidates receive a Permanent Commission in the Indian Army
2. Result Criteria & Selection Stages

The selection process has four clear stages. Each stage filters candidates based on different criteria. Here's how it works:
Stage 1: Initial Shortlisting
Your B.Tech percentage is everything at this stage. The Army shortlists candidates based on their engineering degree marks. Higher the percentage, better your chances. No written test here.

Stage 2: Service Selection Board (SSB) Interview
This is a 5-day intensive assessment at one of the Army's SSB centers. It has four components:
  • Screening: A quick test to assess your basic aptitude and communication
  • Psychological Tests: PPDT, TAT, WAT, SRT
  • GTO Tasks: Group exercises to evaluate leadership, decision-making, and teamwork
  • Interview: A detailed personal interview with SSB officers

Stage 3: Medical Fitness
After SSB success, you undergo a thorough medical examination. The Army has strict fitness standards, and you must clear all tests (vision, hearing, overall health, etc.).

Stage 4: Final Merit List
Your final rank is based on a combination of your SSB score and medical clearance. This determines whether you get the commission and your posting.
3. Scope: Posts, Promotions, Salary & Nature of Work

Your Starting Rank
Once commissioned, you start as a Lieutenant—a junior officer rank with the responsibility of leading troops and managing technical operations.

Promotion Ladder
  • Lieutenant (Lt) — Entry rank, typically 2 years
  • Captain (Capt) — Senior subaltern, typically 3–4 years
  • Major — Company commander, typically 4–5 years
  • Lieutenant Colonel (Lt Col) — Senior field officer
  • Colonel (Col) — Senior management and strategic roles
  • Brigadier and beyond — General staff appointments
Note: Promotions depend on merit, performance, annual reports, and vacancy. Time in rank increases with seniority.

Salary & Perks
Your compensation includes:
  • Basic Pay: Around ₹56,100 as a Lieutenant (as per 7th CPC)
  • Military Service Pay (MSP): Additional ₹5,400
  • Technical Allowances: Extra pay depending on your branch
  • Other Benefits: Housing allowance, medical facilities for family, pension scheme, subsidized canteen, leaves, and post-retirement benefits

Nature of Work
As a TGC officer, your responsibilities typically include:
  • Technical Leadership: Leading technical teams and soldiers under your command
  • Infrastructure & Engineering: Planning, designing, and executing military construction and maintenance projects
  • Equipment Management: Overseeing maintenance and operation of technical equipment
  • Branch-Specific Duties:
    • Civil Engineers: Construction, bridges, roads, fortifications
    • Mechanical Engineers: Vehicle maintenance, equipment management, logistics
    • Electrical/Electronics: Power systems, communication networks, radar operations
    • Signals Officers: Communication infrastructure, cyber-security, IT operations
4. Syllabus & Exam Pattern

Important: There is NO written exam for TGC. Your B.Tech marks get you shortlisted. The real test is the SSB interview.
SSB Testing Components
  • PPDT (Picture Perception & Discussion Test): You see images and discuss interpretations with other candidates. It tests your observation and communication skills.
  • TAT, WAT, SRT (Psychological Tests): These assess your thinking patterns, emotional stability, and how you react under stress.
  • GTO Tasks (Group Testing Officer): Physical and team exercises like group discussion, planning tasks, outdoor obstacles, and leadership scenarios.
  • Interview: One-on-one conversation about your background, motivation, technical knowledge, and suitability for the Army.
5. Selection Process

Here's a step-by-step breakdown of how you go from application to commission:
Step 1: Online Application & Shortlisting

Apply on joinindianarmy.nic.in during the notification period. The Army shortlists candidates based on B.Tech percentage. Only top scorers move forward.


Step 2: 5-Day SSB Interview

You're called to one of the Army's 12 SSB centers across India. Over 5 days, you go through screening, psychological tests, GTO tasks, and personal interviews.


Step 3: Medical Examination

SSB recommended candidates undergo detailed medical checks. You must meet strict Army health standards.


Step 4: Final Merit List & Commission

Your final score is a mix of SSB performance and medical clearance. Top candidates receive the commission and reporting date for training at IMA.

6. Training at Indian Military Academy (IMA)

Once selected, you're posted to the Indian Military Academy (IMA) in Dehradun, one of the world's premier officer training institutions.

Training Duration

The TGC training spans approximately 1 year. It's a mix of physical, technical, and leadership preparation.


What You'll Learn
  • Physical Fitness & Discipline: Morning PT, running, swimming, combat training
  • Weapon Training: Rifle, pistol, explosives, and field tactics
  • Tactics & Field Training: Military strategy, navigation, jungle warfare, crossing obstacles
  • Leadership Classes: Officer etiquette, decision-making, handling soldiers
  • Team Exercises: Outdoor expeditions, group problem-solving, crisis management
  • Technical Instruction: Branch-specific training (Civil, Mechanical, Signals, etc.)
