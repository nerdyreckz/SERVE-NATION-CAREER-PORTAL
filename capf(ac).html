What is CAPF (AC)?

The CAPF (Assistant Commandant) exam is conducted by UPSC to recruit officers for India’s elite paramilitary forces. As an Assistant Commandant, you become a Group-A Gazetted Officer—a frontline leader responsible for national security, law enforcement, and disaster response.

CAPF officers serve in forces like BSF, CRPF, CISF, ITBP, and SSB. This exam is your gateway into one of the most respected uniformed services of India.

Forces Under CAPF

  • BSF: Border Security Force
  • CRPF: Central Reserve Police Force
  • CISF: Central Industrial Security Force
  • ITBP: Indo-Tibetan Border Police
  • SSB: Sashastra Seema Bal

Basic Information

Exam Conducting Body

The exam is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC), once every year.

Eligibility Criteria

Nationality: Must be an Indian citizen.

Age Limit:

  • 20 to 25 years (age relaxations as per govt norms)

Educational Qualification:

  • Graduate from any recognized university

Both men and women can apply, and there is no percentage requirement.

Selection Process

CAPF selection involves 4 stages:

  • Paper 1 – Written Exam (Objective)
  • Paper 2 – Written Exam (Descriptive)
  • PET/PST – Physical Efficiency & Standards
  • Interview / Personality Test

Career as an Assistant Commandant

Starting Rank

Assistant Commandant (AC) – A prestigious Group-A Gazetted Officer rank.

Promotion Structure

Assistant Commandant → Deputy Commandant → Second-in-Command → Commandant → DIG → IG → ADG → DG

Salary & Perks

Basic Pay: ₹56,100 – ₹1,77,500

  • DA, HRA, Transport allowance
  • Free medical & canteen facilities
  • Accommodation & ration allowance
  • Pension and post-retirement benefits
  • Opportunities for UN Peacekeeping missions

Roles & Responsibilities

Assistant Commandants:

  • Lead troops in counter-insurgency missions
  • Border management & national security
  • Disaster relief & rescue operations
  • VIP security, airport security, law enforcement
  • Training and administration

CAPF Syllabus

Paper 1 – General Ability & Intelligence

  • General Science
  • History of India
  • Geography
  • Polity & Economy
  • Current Affairs
  • Mental Ability and Reasoning

Paper 2 – General Studies, Essay & Comprehension

  • Essay writing (security issues, politics, history)
  • Reading comprehension
  • Grammar & language skills

Physical Standards

PET Requirements

  • 100m Race: Men 16 sec | Women 18 sec
  • 800m Race: Men 3 min 45 sec | Women 4 min 45 sec
  • Long Jump: Men 3.5m | Women 3m
  • Shot Put: Men 7.26kg – 4.5m | Women – Not applicable

Training of Assistant Commandants

Training duration is approx 1 year, depending on the force:

  • BSF Academy, Tekanpur
  • CRPF Academy, Kadarpur
  • CISF Academy, Hyderabad
  • ITBP Academy, Mussoorie
  • SSB Academy, Bhopal

Officers undergo weapons training, jungle warfare, law & order management, leadership modules, and physical conditioning.
