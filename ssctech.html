SSC Tech (Army)
Short Service Commission Technical Entry
What is SSC Tech?

SSC Tech is a direct commission scheme for engineering graduates who want to serve as officers in the Indian Army. There's no written exam—your B.Tech percentage is what gets you shortlisted. Both men and women can apply equally.

The Army needs skilled technical officers to manage communications, infrastructure, IT systems, and advanced equipment. If you're an engineer with leadership qualities, this is your pathway to officer rank.
Basic Information You Need to Know

  • Conducting Authority: Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)
  • Entry Type: Technical Officer Commission
  • Frequency: Notifications released twice yearly (typically)
  • Selection Mode: Direct to Service Selection Board (SSB)
  • Training Academy: Officers Training Academy (OTA), Chennai
Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: 20 to 27 years at the time of application
  • Education: B.Tech from AICTE/DEC-approved colleges in branches like Mechanical, Electrical, Electronics, Computer Science, Civil, or Aeronautical
  • Backlogs: No active backlogs at the time of application; cleared backlogs are acceptable
  • Gender: Both men and women are eligible
  • Nationality: Indian citizen
Selection Process

Step 1: Shortlisting — Your B.Tech percentage determines if you move forward. Typically, 65% or higher is preferred.

Step 2: SSB Interview — Selected candidates attend a 5-day Service Selection Board test (psychology, planning, group tasks, and personal interview).

Step 3: Medical Examination — Shortlisted SSB candidates undergo thorough medical and fitness checks.

Step 4: Final Merit List — Results are declared based on overall performance.
Training

Selected officers train at the Officer Training Academy (OTA) in Chennai for approximately 49 weeks. The curriculum includes:
  • Physical training and outdoor survival skills
  • Drill and weapon handling
  • Leadership and decision-making exercises
  • Classroom instruction on military strategy and operations
  • Team-based field activities
  • Tactical deployment scenarios
Career Path & Promotions

You start as a Lieutenant and can progress through: Captain → Major → Lieutenant Colonel → Colonel → Brigadier and beyond.

Technical roles include: Signals and communications, mechanical and electrical maintenance, IT and cyber operations, infrastructure planning, and logistics support.
Salary & Benefits: Starting pay around ₹56,000–62,000 monthly (in-hand), plus allowances, pension, medical benefits, and housing facilities.
