Coast Guard Assistant Commandant
Officer Entry (Group A)
What is the Coast Guard Assistant Commandant Entry?


The Assistant Commandant (AC) is a Group A officer rank in the Indian Coast Guard. This entry gives graduates the chance to become commissioned officers and serve the nation on the seas.

The Indian Coast Guard protects India's coastline, conducts rescue operations, and ensures maritime security. Officers work in dangerous and challenging environments to keep our waters safe.

There are four main branches: General Duty (for all graduates), Technical (for engineers), CPL or Pilot (for pilots), and Law (for law graduates).
Basic Information You Need to Know
The Indian Coast Guard conducts this recruitment. New entries happen once or twice a year.

Selection starts with the CGCAT (Coast Guard Commanding Officer's Assessment Test) written exam. Candidates apply on the official Coast Guard recruitment website.

After selection, officers train at the Indian Naval Academy (INA) in Ezhimala, Kerala. This is where you learn to be a naval officer.
Eligibility Criteria

Basic rules apply to all entries: Indian citizenship, age between 20–24 years, and a valid passport.

General Duty: Any bachelor's degree. Age 20–24 years at the time of application.

Technical Branch: Engineering degree (B.Tech or equivalent). Age 20–24 years.

CPL (Pilot): Any bachelor's degree with flying training. Age 20–24 years.

Law Entry: Bachelor of Laws (LLB) degree. Age 20–24 years.

Both male and female candidates are eligible for all branches.
Exam Pattern (CGCAT)

The CGCAT tests five subjects: English, Mathematics, Reasoning, General Knowledge, and General Science. Questions are multiple-choice, and the exam is moderate in difficulty.

For engineering candidates, there are additional technical questions based on their branch (Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, etc.).

The total test duration is usually 2 hours. Good time management and accuracy are key to scoring well.
Selection Process

CGCAT Written Exam: The first step. This test checks your general knowledge, reasoning, math, and science skills. It shortlists candidates for the next round.

PP&DT (Personality and Physical Demands Test): A test of physical fitness and mental strength. You will complete exercises like running, push-ups, and swimming.

Psychology Tests: These check your mental fitness and how you handle stress. Your thinking patterns and problem-solving skills are evaluated.

Group Tasks: You work with other candidates to solve problems. Officers must show leadership and teamwork.

Personal Interview: A one-on-one talk with senior officers. They ask about your background, goals, and why you want to join the Coast Guard.

Medical Examination: A complete health check. You must be fit and free from any disqualifying medical conditions.

Final Merit List: After all tests, a merit list is released. Selected candidates receive their joining dates for training.
Training

Training at the Indian Naval Academy lasts about one year. You learn naval laws, ship operations, and navigation basics. Physical training and leadership skills are also important.

After INA, you undergo Coast Guard-specific training. This includes seamanship, rescue operations, and emergency response techniques.

You will learn to handle boats, helicopters, and coastal patrol vessels. Safety and quick decision-making are stressed at every step.
Career Path & Salary

You start as Assistant Commandant. With experience and merit, you can be promoted to Deputy Commandant, Commandant, and senior ranks.

Monthly salary is around ₹56,000–₹78,000 (basic pay). Sea duty allowances and flying allowances add to your income.

As an officer, your work includes patrol missions, rescue operations, and enforcing maritime laws. It is challenging but rewarding work.
